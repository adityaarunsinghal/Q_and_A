Instructions

Complete the combo!

In this experiment you will play a communication game with a robot partner!

You will both see a 4 x 4 grid containing cards. For every round of the game, one player (the "seeker") will be given a goal: to find a specific combination of cards in the grid. There will either be 1 or 2 cards in this combo. However, the seeker is trapped in a fog and cannot see where any of the cards are! Thankfully, the other player (the "helper") knows where all the cards are but does not know which ones the seeker is trying to find.

There are two things you need to know to succeed.

  1. Both the seeker and the helper can see the list of possible combos. The seeker will see their target combo highlighted in a green box. These are the cards they are trying to find. The helper knows the seeker is trying to obtain one of the combos in the list but does not know which one.

  1. The seeker is able to ask the helper a question about the location of a card. The seeker must use dropdown menus to complete the question: "Where is the ...?"

    The helper will then see this question and select up to two cards to reveal by clicking on cards, and then on the 'REVEAL' button when ready. They will need to wait until the seeker has sent a question before being able to click anything.

On every round, if the seeker asks a good question and the helper carefully reveals the right cards, it is possible to complete the combo in a single exchange. This is how you get the bonus of $0.05 per round. If the combo is not completed after the first question, or if other cards are revealed, you will not receive the bonus, so pay attention to your choices! There are a total of 6 rounds, and you will switch roles halfway through. After the final round you will answer a few more questions and be on your way.

Finally, please DO NOT refresh the page at any point once you start the game (including the waiting page), or tab away from the page once a partner joins. You not be able to rejoin the game you were in and you may not be able to submit the HIT.
Thank you!
