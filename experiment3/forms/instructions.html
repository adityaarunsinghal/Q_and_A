Instructions

In this experiment, we'll be playing a guessing game. The game has two roles, which will be assigned randomly. Since you will not know which role you will play until the game starts, you will be responsible for understanding the responsibilities and goals of both roles.

If you are the first person to arrive in a game, you must wait shortly for another player to join. Once both players have joined the game, you will see a display like the following:

On the right side of the screen, you will see a workspace filled with various objects and messages. On the left side of the screen, you will see a chat window that will contain messages from the other player.

Guesser instructions

There are four objects hidden behind four gates. These four objects are shown on a wheel. This wheel will spin and pick out one of the objects for you to find. For example, if it lands on the cat, you must try to guess which gate has the cat behind it.

If you had to guess without any further information, you wouldn't stand much of a chance of finding it. Luckily, you are playing with a helper, who knows exactly what is behind each gate. You have the opportunity to ask them a single question before you guess. Just drag the words in the "question box" onto the line to make a question, and then click the 'send' button!

The helper can 'lift' one of the gates, showing you what's behind it. Once you get their answer, you will click on one of the green gates to guess where it is. You will then find out whether you guessed correctly!

Helper instructions

If you are the "helper", you will see a different screen, shown below. You don't know which object the guesser is trying to find, but you know where all of the objects are located! You will watch them drag words up to the line in the question box and then see their question appear in the left side of the screen. Based on this question, you will select one of the four gates and click the "send" button. Sometimes their question will be vague, making it harder to tell what they're looking for, but think about why they might have picked the question they did.

There will be 4 rounds, where the locations of objects are randomly shuffled from round to round. This means that the information the helper gives on one round is completely irrelevant to the locations of items in the next round! Once another player joins, the experiment should take about 7 minutes.
