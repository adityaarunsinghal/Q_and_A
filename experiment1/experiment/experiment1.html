Stanford CoCoLab: Questions and Answers

The actions of asking and answering questions play an important role in our everyday social interactions. It is not always obvious why someone is asking a question, but we intuitively feel like some answers are better than others.

You will see some scenarios where people ask and answer questions. You will then be asked to make some judgements about these scenarios. The HIT should take 3 to 5 minutes. Please pay attention! Thank you!

Instructions

In each of the following trials, you will be asked three things:

  1. First, you will see a short scenario in which someone asks a question. Think about why they might be asking this question and use the provided text box to complete the sentence fragment. For example, if someone says "Do you like cookies?" it might be because they want to buy you one, or they might be trying to find out what kind of cookie you like, for future reference.

  2. Second, you will see a response that someone else has given to this question. Think about why they might have chosen this response and use the provided text box to complete the sentence fragment. For example, they might respond "I like snickerdoodles" because they think the questioner is going to give them one and they want they to choose their favorite, or maybe they want to imply that they don't like other kinds of cookies.

  3. Finally, think about how helpful this answer is for answering the original question and indicate your response on the slider.

Your progress through the experiment is indicated in the top-right corner of the screen. Thank you for your participation!
