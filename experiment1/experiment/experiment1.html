Instructions

People often ask questions to get information. We intuitively feel like some questions are 'better' than others, and some answers are 'better' than others.

In each of the following trials, you will be asked three things:

  1. First, you will see a short scenario in which someone needs some information. Think about what kind of information might be helpful to them and enter some ideas into the text box.

  2. Second, you will see a question that the person in the scenario asks. Think about how good this question is for getting the information you thought about and indicate your response on the slider.

  3. Finally, you will see an answer to their question. Think about how good this answer is with respect to the question and indicate your response on the slider.

Your progress through the experiment is indicated in the top-left corner of the screen. Thank you for your participation!
