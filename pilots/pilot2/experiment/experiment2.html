Stanford CoCoLab: Questions and Answers

The actions of asking and answering questions play an important role in our everyday social interactions. It is not always obvious why someone is asking a question, but we intuitively feel like some answers are better than others.

You will see some scenarios where people ask and answer questions. You will then be asked to make some judgements about these scenarios. The HIT should take 3 to 5 minutes. Please pay attention! Thank you!

Instructions

In each of the following trials, you will first see a scenario and a question. Read these carefully and think about what answers to the question might make sense, given the scenario.

Below this, you will see four possible answers to the question. Use the sliders to indicate how appropriate you think each answer would be. Different answers could be equally appropriate. There will also be a text box below these sliders if you think of an answer that's more appropriate than any of those listed.

Your progress through the experiment is indicated in the top-right corner of the screen. Thank you for your participation!
